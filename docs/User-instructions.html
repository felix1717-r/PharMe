To get started with PharMe, you first need to import your pharmacogenomic (PGx) test data (data about your DNA and medications you are taking) from your data provider.

Tapping the Get data button will forward you to your data provider's sign in.
After successfully importing data, you will get an overview about PharMe's background and capabilities in a short onboarding.

In the initial medication selection you can tell PharMe which medications you are currently taking ‚Äì if your PGx test data includes current medications, they will be pre-selected here.

Later in the app, you will be able to filter for medications you are currently taking; also, some medications can influence your results for other medications. Please take your time here to make the most of PharMe. ‚ò∫Ô∏è
Afterwards, you reach the main app; everything is set up now. (Yay! ü•≥)

The first page you will see is the Report screen; here you can see the results from your your PGx test by gene (we know the names are cryptic, but you will get to know them sooner as you think!).

Under each gene name, your phenotype is depicted. The phenotype tells you what the consequence of your genetic variant is; e.g., as a CYP2C19 Intermediate Metabolizer, your CYP2C19 enzyme ‚Äì the protein that is encoded by the CYP2C19 gene ‚Äì works a bit slower than expected.

Selecting a gene will take you to its detail page, with further information about your result and medications that are influenced by this gene. For technical terms (like "genotype"), you can always find explanations by tapping on the question mark.
You can also view your PGx results by medications on the Medications screen. Here, you can search for medications by their generic name, brand names, or class. You can also filter the list by medications you are currently taking and by guideline result (‚ùå consider alternatives, ‚ö†Ô∏è use with caution, and ‚úÖ standard precautions).

Selecting a medication (also on the gene detail page) leads to the medication's page with further details.

Here you see some general information about the medication and a short statement what PGx guidelines say about your DNA and this medication. For more information, the respective guideline is linked at the bottom of each medication page.
The FAQ page collects information that might be useful for you.

If you want to view the onboarding again or review our Terms of Use or Privacy Policy, you can find the information you need on the More tab.

Please be careful when deleting your data; this will reset the app in its initial state and also delete your PGx test data. It might not be possible to import your data again, if you lost your credentials or your data provider deleted your data.

We hope by using PharMe you will learn useful information about your DNA and medications! Have fun! üí´
